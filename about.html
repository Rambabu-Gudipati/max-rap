MAX-RAP: Maximizing the Recyclability of Asphalt Pavements

A Joint Indo-German Research Initiative

Objective

To transform reclaimed asphalt pavement (RAP) into a fully reusable, high-performance material by developing new scientific frameworks for binder-level recyclability, material compatibility, and industrial-scale recovery processes.

Key Goals

  • Quantify RAP binder recyclability using chemical and rheological characterization (FTIR, TLC, DSR).
  • Develop alternatives to virgin bitumen and rejuvenators using refinery by-products (e.g., slop oil, heavy fractions).
  • Isolate and evaluate RAP components by decoupling binder-level behavior from mix-level uncertainty.
  • Model viscoelastic behavior under temperature and pressure to design performance-optimized RAP blends.
  • Create industrial protocols to enable selective recovery and maximize reuse of RAP fractions.

Research Highlights

  • - Use of pressure–temperature rheology to assess binder aging and recovery.
  • - Development of a Recyclability Index and Compatibility Framework to inform mix design.
  • - Implementation of a binder isolation technique to characterize intrinsic performance.

Expected Impact

  • Enable 100% RAP utilization in select highway applications.
  • Support climate-neutral road construction practices.
  • -Provide an open-source framework for standardizing RAP reuse in India and Germany.

Project Consortium

  • India: IIT Madras, PSG Tech, Bharat Petroleum Corporation Ltd.
  • Germany: Technische Universität Dresden, Heitkamp Straßen- und Tiefbau GmbH.
  • Supported by: Indo-German Science & Technology Centre.
